How It Works

The Algorithm Behind the Price Tag

Every price you see is the result of a complex calculation happening in milliseconds, weighing dozens of data points about you and the market.

01

Data Collection

Algorithms gather data from your browsing history, cookies, device type, operating system, location, time of day, and even how many times you've viewed a product.

02

Profile Analysis

Machine learning models create a consumer profile that estimates your willingness to pay, brand loyalty, price sensitivity, and purchasing urgency based on behavioral patterns.

03

Price Delivery

The algorithm sets a personalized price in real-time ‚Äî different users may see different prices for the exact same product at the exact same moment, maximizing revenue per customer.

Quick Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Algorithmic pricing (also called dynamic or personalized pricing) is the practice of using computer algorithms and data analytics to automatically adjust prices in real-time based on factors like consumer behavior, market conditions, competitor pricing, and individual user data.

In most jurisdictions, dynamic pricing is legal as long as it doesn't discriminate based on protected characteristics like race, gender, or religion. However, regulations vary by country and are still evolving as the technology advances.

Use incognito/private browsing, clear your cookies regularly, compare prices across multiple devices, use price comparison tools, and consider using a VPN to mask your location. Check out our FAQ page for more detailed tips.
