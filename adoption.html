Growing Your Family Through Adoption

Adoption is one of the most meaningful ways to grow your family. Whether you're a stepparent seeking to legally adopt your spouse's child, a grandparent or relative caring for a child in need, or pursuing an independent adoption, the process requires careful attention to Colorado's legal requirements.

At Kovarsky Law, we guide families through every step of the adoption process, ensuring all legal requirements are met and helping you navigate any challenges that arise.

Our goal is to help you finalize your adoption so you can focus on what matters most—building your family.

Types of Adoption We Handle

Stepparent Adoption

When a stepparent wants to legally adopt their spouse's child, creating a permanent legal parent-child relationship. This typically requires consent from the non-custodial biological parent or termination of their parental rights.

Relative/Kinship Adoption

Grandparents, aunts, uncles, or other relatives who have been caring for a child may seek to formalize the relationship through adoption. This provides legal security and permanency for both the child and the family.

Independent/Private Adoption

Adoptions arranged directly between birth parents and adoptive parents, without an agency. These require careful attention to legal requirements including proper consent, background checks, and home studies.

Adult Adoption

Colorado allows for the adoption of adults in certain circumstances. This can formalize a parent-child relationship that has existed for years or create inheritance rights and other legal connections.

The Adoption Process

1

Initial Consultation

2

Filing the Petition

We prepare and file the adoption petition with the court, along with all required documentation and supporting materials.

3

Home Study & Background Checks

Most adoptions require a home study by a licensed agency and background checks for all adults in the household.

4

Finalization Hearing

The final step is a court hearing where the judge reviews the case and issues the adoption decree, legally establishing the parent-child relationship.

Common Questions

How long does an adoption take?

Timeline varies by adoption type. Stepparent adoptions with consent can be finalized in a few months. Contested adoptions or those requiring termination of parental rights take longer, sometimes a year or more.

Do both biological parents need to consent?

Generally, yes. However, parental rights can be terminated by the court if a parent has abandoned the child, failed to support the child, or is unfit. We can help navigate situations where consent may not be required.

What is a home study?

A home study is an evaluation conducted by a licensed social worker or agency. It includes interviews, home visits, and background checks to ensure the adoptive home is safe and suitable for the child.

Can an adoption be reversed?

Once finalized, adoptions are very difficult to reverse. Colorado allows birth parents a limited time to revoke consent before finalization, but once the adoption decree is entered, it is permanent in most circumstances.
