üìà Slope Interpretation ‚ñº

They give you an equation like ≈∑ = 12x + 5. The 12 is the slope. They want a sentence, not a calculation.

Say this
"For each 1 [x-unit] increase in [x], [y] increases/decreases by [slope], on average."
"on average" is REQUIRED ‚Äî you lose points without it!

Use actual variable names, never just "x" and "y".

Ex: ≈∑ = 12x + 5 (x=customers, y=sales)
"For each additional customer, daily sales increase by $12, on average."
üîó Correlation (r) & r¬≤ ‚ñº

r = how closely dots follow a straight line. Always between ‚àí1 and +1.

‚àí1 ‚â§ r ‚â§ +1
|r|Strength

üìâ Regression & Prediction ‚ñº
≈∑ = b‚ÇÄ + b‚ÇÅ(x)

≈∑ = predicted y ¬∑ b‚ÇÅ = slope ¬∑ b‚ÇÄ = y-intercept (predicted y when x=0, only meaningful if x=0 makes sense)

To predict: just plug x into the equation.

Ex: ≈∑ = 2.73(Age) + 67.5, predict for Age=10
≈∑ = 2.73(10) + 67.5 = 94.8 kg
NEVER predict outside the data range ‚Äî that's extrapolation!
Extrapolation answer
"No ‚Äî predicting at x=[val] would be extrapolation because it's outside the observed range ([min] to [max])."

One use of a regression line = estimate the change in y for a one-unit change in x (that's the slope). This wording is on the exam!
