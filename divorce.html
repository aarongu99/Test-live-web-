Understanding Your Divorce Options

Divorce is never easy, but having the right legal representation can make a significant difference in your experience and outcome. At Kovarsky Law, we understand the emotional and financial toll that divorce can take, and we're committed to guiding you through this process with clarity, compassion, and strategic focus.

Colorado is a "no-fault" divorce state, meaning neither spouse needs to prove wrongdoing to obtain a divorce. This approach aims to reduce conflict and allow couples to focus on resolving practical matters like property division, spousal support, and arrangements for children.

Whether you're considering an uncontested divorce where both parties agree on major issues, or facing a contested divorce that requires litigation, we provide the experienced representation you need to protect your interests and secure your future.

Types of Divorce We Handle

Uncontested Divorce

When both spouses agree on all major issues including property division, parenting time, and support, an uncontested divorce offers a faster, less expensive path to dissolution. We ensure all agreements are properly documented and legally sound.

Contested Divorce

When spouses cannot agree on key issues, contested divorce may require negotiation, mediation, or court intervention. We advocate vigorously for your interests while working toward efficient resolution whenever possible.

High-Asset Divorce

Complex financial situations require careful attention to asset valuation, business interests, retirement accounts, and tax implications. We work with financial experts when necessary to ensure accurate assessment and fair division.

Divorce with Children

When children are involved, additional considerations include parenting plans, decision-making responsibilities, parenting time schedules, and child support. We prioritize arrangements that serve your children's best interests while protecting your parental rights.

Common Questions

How long does a divorce take in Colorado?

Colorado requires a minimum 91-day waiting period from the date of service. Uncontested divorces may be finalized shortly after this period, while contested divorces can take several months to over a year depending on complexity.

How is property divided?

Colorado follows "equitable distribution," meaning marital property is divided fairly, though not necessarily equally. Factors include each spouse's economic circumstances, contributions to the marriage, and the value of property assigned to each party.

Will I have to go to court?

Many divorces settle without a trial. However, some court appearances may be necessary for status conferences or if issues cannot be resolved through negotiation. We prepare you thoroughly for any required appearances.

Can I get alimony (spousal maintenance)?

Spousal maintenance may be awarded based on factors including marriage length, each spouse's income and earning capacity, and the standard of living during the marriage. Colorado has guidelines that provide a starting point for calculating maintenance.
