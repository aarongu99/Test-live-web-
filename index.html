üîë Key Concepts to Know Cold

1. Types of Variables

Categorical = categories/labels (favorite color, gender, zip code)
Quantitative = numerical measurements (height, salary, number of kids)

2. Describing Distributions (Shape, Center, Spread, Outliers)

Shape: Symmetric (bell), Right-skewed (tail right, mean > median), Left-skewed (tail left, mean < median)
Center: Mean (symmetric data) or Median (skewed/outliers)
Spread: Std Dev (symmetric) or IQR (skewed/outliers)
Outliers: Below Q1 ‚àí 1.5¬∑IQR or Above Q3 + 1.5¬∑IQR

3. Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7)

Only applies to bell-shaped (symmetric) distributions.
‚Ä¢ 68% within 1 SD of mean
‚Ä¢ 95% within 2 SD of mean ‚Üí 2.5% in each tail
‚Ä¢ 99.7% within 3 SD of mean

4. Z-Scores

z = (x ‚àí mean) / SD
Interpretation: "The value is |z| standard deviations above/below the mean."
Unusual if |z| > 2

5. Linear Regression & Correlation

r = correlation (‚àí1 to +1): direction & strength of LINEAR relationship
r¬≤ = coefficient of determination: "___% of variation in Y is explained by the linear relationship with X"
≈∑ = b‚ÇÄ + b‚ÇÅx: slope b‚ÇÅ = "for each 1-unit increase in X, Y changes by b‚ÇÅ on average"
Extrapolation = predicting outside the range of observed X values ‚Äî don't do it!

6. Boxplots

5-number summary: Min, Q1, Median, Q3, Max
IQR = Q3 ‚àí Q1
Fences: Q1 ‚àí 1.5¬∑IQR and Q3 + 1.5¬∑IQR
Outliers are plotted as individual dots beyond the fences.

7. Comparing Distributions

Compare on 4 features: Shape, Center, Spread, Outliers ‚Äî always IN CONTEXT.

Q8 ‚Äî Interpreting Regression Equation

Q11 ‚Äî Shape from Mean vs Median

  • Symmetric

Q12 ‚Äî Empirical Rule (% between 60 and 80)
Mean = 80, SD = 10. What percentage of scores lies between 60 and 80?
  • 47.5%
  • 34%
  • 68%
  • 95%
60 is 2 SDs below mean (z = ‚àí2). 80 is the mean (z = 0). From the empirical rule, 95% is between mean ¬± 2 SD. Half of 95% = 47.5% is between the mean and 2 SDs below.

  • Midrange
  • Mean
  • Median
Median is resistant to outliers and skewness. The mean gets pulled by extreme values.

Q17 ‚Äî Resistant to Outliers
Which statistic is unaffected by outliers?
  • Mean
  • Interquartile Range
  • Standard deviation
  • Range
IQR only uses Q1 and Q3 (middle 50%), so extreme values don't affect it. Mean, SD, and Range all change with outliers.
Q18 ‚Äî Finding the Median

  • 76
  • 77
  • 80

Q19 ‚Äî Not Symmetric Indicator

  • The range is larger than the IQR
  • The mean is much smaller than the median
  • There are no outliers
If mean ‚â† median ‚Üí skewed ‚Üí not symmetric. Mean much smaller than median = left-skewed.
Q20 ‚Äî Outlier Effect on Correlation
What is the effect of an outlier on a correlation value?
  • Always closer to ‚àí1
  • Always closer to 1
  • Always closer to 0
  • No effect
Outliers typically weaken the linear pattern, pulling r closer to 0. (In rare cases an outlier can inflate r, but the general rule tested in intro stats is that outliers weaken correlation.)
Q21 ‚Äî Use of Regression Line

The slope of a regression line tells us the estimated change in Y for each 1-unit increase in X.
